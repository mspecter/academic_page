$ Whoami

I am a first-year PhD student in the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science department at MIT. I am mainly interested in the intersection of systems security, cryptography, economics, and public policy. While my research advisors are David Clark and Danny Weitzner, I also hang around with Gerry Sussman, Hal Abelson, and Jeff Schiller in MIT CSAIL's Internet Policy Research Initiative.

My overall goal is to make evil more expensive by developing new secure systems, crafting regulation, or otherwise imposing economic pressures on the bad guys.

Prior to joining MIT as a graduate student, I worked at MIT's Lincoln Laboratory in the Cyber Security and Information Sciences division doing research in computer security, malware analysis, reverse engineering, and vulnerability discovery.
