$ Whoami

I am a first-year PhD student in the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science department at MIT. I am mainly interested in the intersection of systems security, cryptography, economics, and public policy. While my research advisors are David Clark and Danny Weitzner, I also hang around with Gerry Sussman, Hal Abelson, and Jeff Schiller in MIT CSAIL's Internet Policy Research Initiative.

Generally, my goal is to make being evil on the Internet more expensive either by developing new secure systems, crafting regulation, or otherwise imposing economic pressures on bad guys.

Prior to joining MIT as a graduate student, I worked at MIT's Lincoln Laboratory in their cybersecurity division doing research in computer security, malware analysis, reverse engineering, and vulnerability discovery.
